The collection of reports, speeches and essays by Dimitroff form-
ing the content of The United Front is large enough (624k) to war-
rent dividing the file into two parts for those having modest capacity computers. These readers can download each of the two separate sec-
tions (via the table of contents or at the bottom of the page) into which the text has been divided. Those having computers with plenty of RAM (and a fast modem) can download the entire file and will need to allocate at least an additional 2MB of RAM to the browser's "sug-
gested size" (e. g., about 7MB for Netscape 2 and Internet Explorer 2) to view the text. The following table of contents for The United Front will give you some idea of what is contained in each section. I have included the complete index for the book in each section, although it should be understood it is often the case that page references are for pages in the other part. To use the index, merely highlight the name of the entry, load it into the "FIND" command of your browser, and go from there; the page numbers are included merely to give you a sense of how (in)frequently a topic appears and in which of the two parts it appears.
