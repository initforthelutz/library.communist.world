      • May 1, 1997

          I had originally intended to debut From Marx to Mao on June 1, 1997, at which time there would be roughly twenty texts in the Lenin Library. However, May Day seemed a more appropriate date since Capital, Vol. II was already completed and sitting around. As such, I moved everything up by one month and hurried to have a little something for each of the respective Libraries. Since I have a number of texts from Lenin in a state of near completion, requiring only a little re-coding for greater compatibility with earlier versions of Internet browers and some cosmetic touch-ups, these texts will be coming on-line throughout the month of May.

      June 29, 1997

          I don't have a large number of texts planned for July (see below). Rather, the time has come for a little house-keeping. My intent is to update many of the files, i.e., add hypertext links, to reflect the additional texts in the various collections. Above all, I hope to update the "Reading Guide".

          Also, the "Which Browser?" section will be updated, for "low-end" Mac users, to reflect the release of Internet Explorer 3.01 (the post-beta release) for 68k platforms. My initial experience with it indicates, with respect to this site, that its "table" features (the only feature that counts here) offers no improvement over version 2.0x.

          The reader may have noticed that this "What's Next?" feature always lists far fewer texts than actually end up appearing when I post new files. There are too many variables (primarily, the time I have available, the quality of the scans, and, of course, the condition of my "hacked" computer) that work against any accurate planning on my part. I would like to be able to promise in advance what I end up delivering, but the best I can do is deliver what I promise. As such, it appears that "What's Next?" will always list fewer texts than will actually be posted.

      December 31, 1997

          The new year in will bring a couple of additional "features" to FROM MARX TO MAO. With any luck at all, by the first week of February there will be a search facility available for readers. This effort to beef-up the site with some "web-tech" will undoubtedly be very shakey at first, at least until I learn what I'm supposed to do to make it function properly. Once this feature is in place, FROM MARX TO MAO will begin to carry Spanish versions of some of the classic texts of communist literature. Unfortunately, there is no way I can provide Spanish editions of all the documents currently available at this site, but the apparent paucity of such texts available in Spanish on the Internet needs to be corrected. For a variety of technical reasons this will be slow-going, but FROM MARX TO MAO is making a committment to begin to fill this void in the coming months.
